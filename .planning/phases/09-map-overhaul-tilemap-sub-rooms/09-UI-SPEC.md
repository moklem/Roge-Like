## Spacing Scale

The standard 8-point web spacing scale does not apply to tile-based game geometry.
Instead, all spatial dimensions are expressed in **tile units** (1 tile = 16 px).

| Token | Tile Value | Pixel Value | Usage |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| tile | 1 tile | 16 px | Atomic grid unit — all positions, sizes, and offsets snap to this |
| wall-thickness | 2 tiles | 32 px | Minimum solid wall depth — thick enough to block vision and movement |
| doorway-width | 3 tiles | 48 px | Minimum passable exit width — fits 2 players side-by-side |
| doorway-blocked | 3 tiles | 48 px | Blocked exit passage width (same as open; wall tiles fill it until cleared) |
| corridor-width | 4–6 tiles | 64–96 px | Walkable corridor minimum — Altstadt min 4, ERBA/Burg min 6 |
| obstacle-min | 3×3 tiles | 48×48 px | Smallest obstacle block (building footprint minimum) |
| spawn-margin | 4 tiles | 64 px | Minimum clearance between player spawn point and nearest wall tile |
| camera-tile | 16 px | 1:1 | Tile size equals 1 world pixel — no sub-pixel correction needed |

Exceptions:
- Sub-room outer boundary walls are 1 tile thick (16 px) where they form the edge of the playable area — sufficient because players never reach the absolute edge.
- Connector corridors: width is exactly 6 tiles (96 px) — wide enough to prevent feeling cramped during the walk between locations.

Source: CONTEXT.md D-18 (tile size 16×16 px, sub-rooms in tile-grid units); default derived from gameplay readability requirements.

## Sub-Room Dimension Standards

All sub-rooms are rectangular. Dimensions are expressed as W×H in tile units.

### Room 1 — ERBA (Roguelike Modern City tileset)

| Sub-Room | Tiles (W×H) | Pixels (W×H) | Character | Walkable Area |
|----------|------------|--------------|-----------|---------------|
| SR-1 (intro) | 50×35 | 800×560 | Open park — lightest density | ~75% |
| SR-2 | 55×38 | 880×608 | Open park + first building blocks | ~70% |
| SR-3 | 60×40 | 960×640 | Mixed asphalt/grass, 3–4 obstacles | ~65% |
| SR-4 | 60×42 | 960×672 | Denser buildings, narrowing paths | ~60% |
| SR-5 | 65×44 | 1040×704 | Pre-corridor — most obstacles; heaviest density before connector | ~55% |
| Connector (SR-6) | 80×10 | 1280×160 | Straight horizontal road corridor, no enemies | ~85% (full width minus walls) |

ERBA sub-rooms feel island-like. Where the outer boundary wall meets the bottom/left edges, place a 2-tile-wide strip of a visually distinct tile (e.g., dark stone or dark asphalt edge) to suggest the Regnitz river banks without needing water animation.

### Room 2 — Bamberg Altstadt (Roguelike Modern City tileset)

| Sub-Room | Tiles (W×H) | Pixels (W×H) | Character | Walkable Area |
|----------|------------|--------------|-----------|---------------|
| SR-1 | 55×38 | 880×608 | Entry square — slightly open | ~55% |
| SR-2 | 55×40 | 880×640 | First narrow street corridors | ~45% |
| SR-3 | 60×42 | 960×672 | Dense block grid, multiple choke points | ~40% |
| SR-4 | 60×44 | 960×704 | Tightest street layout — punishes clustering | ~38% |
| SR-5 | 65×45 | 1040×720 | Large courtyard blocked by building mass | ~42% |
| Connector (SR-6) | 80×10 | 1280×160 | Stone path corridor (transitions to Burg visual) | ~85% |

Altstadt walkable area must be noticeably tighter than ERBA at every equivalent sub-room number. The contrast between ROOM-01 (open) and ROOM-02 (tight) must be visible within 2 seconds of entering.

### Room 3 — Burg Altenburg (Tiny Dungeon tileset, with 1-Bit Pack fallback for wall variety)

| Sub-Room | Tiles (W×H) | Pixels (W×H) | Character | Walkable Area |
|----------|------------|--------------|-----------|---------------|
| SR-1 | 55×40 | 880×640 | Outer fortress courtyard | ~60% |
| SR-2 | 55×40 | 880×640 | Inner gatehouse passage | ~50% |
| SR-3 | 60×42 | 960×672 | Second courtyard with tower obstacles | ~55% |
| SR-4 | 65×44 | 1040×704 | Approach to keep — dense tower blocks | ~48% |
| SR-5 (boss arena) | 70×50 | 1120×800 | Open arena — boss fight; no normal obstacles in center | ~70% center clear, walls thick |

Boss arena (SR-5 Room 3) is intentionally more open than surrounding sub-rooms to allow boss movement patterns. A thick perimeter wall (2–3 tiles) encloses the arena. No interior obstacle blocks in the center 40×30 tile region.

Source: CONTEXT.md D-09, D-18, specifics note (5 sub-rooms, ~1 min combat each, escalating density). Dimensions are defaults from the discretion area — planner adjusts per OSM abstraction.

## Color / Visual Identity Contract

This is a game world visual layer, not a web UI. The 60/30/10 split is expressed as tile coverage ratios per sub-room.

### Room 1 — ERBA: Dystopian Overgrown City

| Role | Tile Category | Coverage | Visual Intent |
|------|--------------|----------|---------------|
| Dominant (60%) | Grass floor tiles (rows 6–7 of Modern City) | ~60% of floor area | Vegetation reclaiming the city — feels alive but decayed |
| Secondary (30%) | Asphalt/concrete floor tiles (row 3 of Modern City) | ~30% of floor area | Road remnants, cracked pavement beneath the growth |
| Accent (10%) | Brick wall + building roof obstacle tiles (rows 0–2) | ~10% of total area (walls + obstacles) | Solid blockers that define the space |

ERBA floor mix rule: For every 3 floor tiles placed, 2 are grass variants and 1 is asphalt. Implement by using alternating atlas coords in the floor rect iteration, or by placing a second floor pass that overwrites ~30% of grass tiles with asphalt.

### Room 2 — Bamberg Altstadt: Urban Street Grid

| Role | Tile Category | Coverage | Visual Intent |
|------|--------------|----------|---------------|
| Dominant (60%) | Asphalt/concrete floor tiles (row 3 of Modern City) | ~60% of floor area | City street surface — harder, more urban than ERBA |
| Secondary (30%) | Building obstacle/roof tiles (rows 0–2 of Modern City) | ~30% of total area | Dense building mass — significantly more obstacles than ERBA |
| Accent (10%) | Grass tiles (rows 6–7) | ~10% of floor area | Sparse overgrowth — city has less vegetation than ERBA |

Altstadt floor mix rule: For every 10 floor tiles placed, 1 is a grass tile. Grass appears in corners and gaps only — not in the main walkway.

### Room 3 — Burg Altenburg: Stone Fortress

| Role | Tile Category | Coverage | Visual Intent |
|------|--------------|----------|---------------|
| Dominant (60%) | Stone floor tiles (row 1 of Tiny Dungeon) | ~60% of total area | Fortress courtyard ground — cold and uniform |
| Secondary (30%) | Castle stone wall + obstacle tiles (row 0 of Tiny Dungeon) | ~30% of total area | Thick fortress walls and tower blocks |
| Accent (10%) | Darker stone variant tiles | ~10% of floor area | Surface texture variation — worn areas, shadows |

No grass tiles in Room 3. No asphalt tiles in Room 3. Visual identity must be completely distinct from Rooms 1 and 2.

### Connector Corridors

| Connector | Floor | Wall | Visual Rule |
|-----------|-------|------|-------------|
| ERBA → Altstadt | Road tiles (row 15 of Modern City) | Brick tiles (row 0) | Straight road; 1-tile center line stripe |
| Altstadt → Burg | Stone path (row 1 of Tiny Dungeon) | Castle stone (row 0 of Tiny Dungeon) | Transitional feel — city giving way to fortress |

Source: CONTEXT.md D-14–D-16, D-19; CONTEXT.md specifics ("dystopian overgrown", "grass tiles mixed INTO asphalt").

## Camera Contract

Source: CONTEXT.md D-01–D-03, RESEARCH.md Pattern 2.

| Property | Value | Source |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Node | `Camera2D` child of `Player.tscn` | D-01 |
| Enabled for | `is_multiplayer_authority()` peer only | D-01 |
| Network sync | None — camera position is purely local | D-01 |
| Zoom | `Vector2(1.5, 1.5)` (recommended starting value — planner adjusts for playability) | D-02 (zoom TBD) |
| Smoothing | `position_smoothing_enabled = true`, `position_smoothing_speed = 5.0` | Default — adjust if camera feels laggy |
| Limit method | `limit_left`, `limit_top`, `limit_right`, `limit_bottom` set in pixel coordinates | D-03 |
| Limit update trigger | Called by `RoomBuilder.build_sub_room()` on every sub-room entry, for all peers | D-03 |
| Limit formula | `limit_left = sub_room_origin_x * TILE_SIZE`, `limit_right = (sub_room_origin_x + width_tiles) * TILE_SIZE`, same for top/bottom | RESEARCH.md Pattern 2 |
| Black void prevention | Camera limits must be set BEFORE the camera is enabled or the first `_process()` runs | Pitfall 3 |
| Sub-room origin | All sub-rooms place at TileMap origin `Vector2i(0,0)` — limits always start at `0, 0` unless multiple sub-rooms coexist in same TileMap | Default |

**Zoom rationale:** At zoom 1.0, a 60×40 tile sub-room (960×640 px) exactly fills an 800×600 window — no scrolling occurs. Zoom 1.5 makes each tile 24 px on screen, reduces the visible area to ~640×427 px world space, and makes the scrolling camera system actually useful. The planner should test zoom 1.5, 2.0, and 2.5 and pick the most readable value. Document the chosen value in the PLAN.md.

## Exit Passage Visual Contract

The blocked exit is a critical interaction state — players must be able to read "this is the way out, but it's blocked."

### Blocked State (sub-room not yet cleared)

- Exit passage width: exactly 3 tiles (48 px)
- Exit passage tiles: same wall tile as surrounding perimeter wall — no visual distinction from wall
- The blocked exit is NOT visually marked with a special tile, color, or indicator
- Players discover the exit is walkable ONLY after enemies are cleared (tile disappears)
- Rationale: Keeps visual simplicity; avoids UI overlay in the game world; consistent with roguelike "find the exit" convention

### Open State (after `_open_exit_passage()` RPC)

- `erase_cell()` removes the 3 wall tiles at exit position
- Immediately replace with the room's floor tile type via `set_cell()` — do NOT leave empty cells (empty cells show the TileMap background, which is transparent / black)
- The opened gap is self-evidently navigable — floor tiles continue through, wall tiles are gone
- No animation, no particle effect — tile swap is instantaneous on all peers simultaneously

### Exit Direction Convention

| Room | Default Exit Direction | Wall Side |
|------|----------------------|-----------|
| ERBA SR-1 through SR-4 | Right (`Vector2i(1, 0)`) | Right wall |
| ERBA SR-5 → Connector | Right | Right wall |
| Altstadt SR-1 through SR-4 | Right | Right wall |
| Altstadt SR-5 → Connector | Right | Right wall |
| Burg SR-1 through SR-4 | Right | Right wall |
| Burg SR-5 (boss arena) | N/A — boss cleared → loop end | — |

All exits face right for consistency. The planner may vary direction for specific sub-rooms if OSM abstraction suggests a different orientation — document any deviation in `RoomLayouts.gd` comments.

Source: CONTEXT.md D-08; RESEARCH.md Pattern 3.

## Visual Consistency Rules

### Wall Thickness

- Perimeter walls: 2 tiles thick (32 px) on all outer edges of every sub-room
- Internal divider walls (between obstacle groups): 1 tile thick (16 px) minimum
- Corner fills: Required — no diagonal gaps at wall corners (fill with wall tile at every corner intersection)
- Door gap is the ONLY intentional break in the perimeter wall

### Obstacle Density by Sub-Room Number

| Sub-Room # | Obstacle Density | Obstacle Block Sizes |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Light (5–8% of floor area) | Small blocks: 3×3 to 4×4 tiles |
| 2 | Moderate (8–12% of floor area) | 3×3 to 5×5 tile blocks |
| 3 | Medium (12–18% of floor area) | 4×6 to 6×8 tile blocks |
| 4 | Heavy (18–25% of floor area) | 5×8 to 8×10 tile blocks |
| 5 | Dense (25–35% of floor area) | 6×8 to 10×12 tile blocks; multiple grouped |
| Boss arena (SR-5 R3) | Perimeter only — center clear | 40×30 tile center zone has NO obstacles |

These are starting targets. The planner adjusts per actual OSM abstraction geometry.

### ERBA-Specific Rules

- Sub-rooms should feel like a river island — rectangular shape with implied water at boundaries
- Grass/asphalt mix: 2:1 ratio in favor of grass (see Color Contract above)
- Obstacles represent building footprints seen from directly above — rectangular blocks only
- Avoid dense obstacle grids — ERBA must remain visually open even at SR-5

### Altstadt-Specific Rules

- Corridors between building blocks must be exactly 4–6 tiles wide — never wider
- Building blocks (obstacles) are larger and more numerous than ERBA
- The overall shape of obstacle placement should suggest a medieval street grid: long parallel building rows with narrow gaps between them
- At least 2 corridors in each Altstadt sub-room must be only 4 tiles wide (64 px) — creates the "punishes clustering" feel from ROOM-02

### Burg-Specific Rules

- Sub-rooms should feel like a sequence of courtyards enclosed by fortress walls
- Walls between courtyards are 2–3 tiles thick — heavier than ERBA/Altstadt
- Tower obstacle blocks are square (4×4 or 5×5 tiles) — represent turret tops
- The boss arena (SR-5) has a clear 40×30 tile central space with only perimeter walls — boss fight requires room to maneuver

Source: CONTEXT.md D-19–D-20, specifics ("ERBA island", "Altstadt medieval street grid", "Burg multiple courtyards").

## Interaction Contract (Multiplayer)

| Event | Trigger | Visual Result | Authority |
|-------|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Sub-room entry | `_transition_to_sub_room(N)` RPC called on all peers | TileMap cleared and rebuilt from layout data; camera limits updated | Host calls RPC; all peers execute locally |
| Sub-room clear | All enemies in current sub-room dead | Exit passage opens (3 wall tiles replaced by floor tiles) | Host detects, calls `_open_exit_passage()` RPC |
| Exit walk-through | Player `CharacterBody2D` enters exit gap | Area2D or trigger zone advances to next sub-room | Host detects body-entered signal |
| Location transition | Connector corridor end reached | Fade → scene change to next location | Existing `_transition_to_room()` RPC reused |
| Camera limits update | Each sub-room entry | `Camera2D.limit_*` updated to sub-room pixel bounds | Local (each peer sets its own camera) |
| TileMap collision toggle | Room shown / hidden | `TileMap.collision_layer = 1` (active room) or `0` (hidden room) | Host during `_transition_to_room()` |

Source: CONTEXT.md D-08, D-11, D-12; RESEARCH.md Pattern 3, Pitfall 4.
